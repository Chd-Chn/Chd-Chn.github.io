These are AI extracted statistics from Vietnam’s National Water Resources Report 2016 to 2021(Ministry of Resources and Environment), highlighting surface water and groundwater availability, future demand, and key environmental challenges.

2. Current Status of Water Resources

  • Vietnam has 1,107 rain measurement stations | 213 surface meteorological stations | 865 rain gauge stations | 28 oceanographic stations.
  • 354 hydrological stations and 46 centrally managed water stations are operating across the country.
  • Infrastructure includes radar-equipped hydrological stations and 1 global climate station.
  • Stations are concentrated along key river basins: Hong – Thai Binh, Ma, Ca, Vu Gia – Thu Bon, Ba, Dong Nai, Cuu Long.
  • Among 46 central surface water stations, 23 are independent and 23 are part of hydrological stations.
  • Regional distribution includes Northwest (3), Northeast (4), South Central (2), Central Highlands (9), and Mekong Delta (5).
  • As of 2021, there are 412 monitoring points and 805 projects under central management.
  • Monitoring coverage:
    • Hong – Thai Binh: 87 points, 156 works (55%)
    • Se San: 41 points, 46 works (85%)
    • Dong Nai: 51 points, 106 works (61%)
    • Cuu Long: 49 points, 218 works (75%)
    • Full coverage (100%) in Ca, Gianh, Thach Han, Nhat Le, and Vu Gia – Thu Bon basins
  • No monitoring in: Bang Giang – Ky Cung, Kon Ha Thanh, Cai Nha Trang, Ve, and Quang Ninh river group.
  • National average rainfall: ~1,990 mm/year, with significant seasonal and regional variation.
  • Highest recorded (2016–2021): Bac Quang station: 4,756 mm; Lowest: Bao Lac station: 1,203 mm.
  • Rainy season by region:
    • North: Late April – Sept/Oct (80% of annual rain)
    • North Central: May – Nov/Dec
    • South Central: Aug – Dec
    • Central Highlands: Apr/May – Oct/Nov
    • Mekong Delta: May – Nov

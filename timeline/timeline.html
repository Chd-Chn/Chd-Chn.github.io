Timeline of Significant Events

A history of hunger: why Vietnam prioritizes food security

Poor production levels (1953–1982) Poor production under collectivist farming and the collapse of collectivism.
Strain from military demands (1955–1975) The ‘5% plot’ scheme required farmers to set aside land for military needs during the war.
Ineffective state-controlled food policies (1975–1986) The Food Obligation policy required farmers to sell grain to the state below market prices.
Social equity reforms tied to food access (1975–late 1980s) The Ho Khau system and land redistribution sought to address food access through wealth redistribution.
Recovery from hunger through market reform (1986–1992) Doi Moi reforms allowed farmers to operate freely on the open market, greatly boosting food production.
Continued oversight through land rights (1993 onward) The Red Book (Land Use Rights Certificate) gave the state control over land while promoting market-driven agricultural growth.
Global trade and credibility (1995–2007) Vietnam joined ASEAN and WTO, cementing its role as a global player in agriculture.
